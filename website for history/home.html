4 Types of transportations that were used when British colonized India

Transport System #1: The Railways

The Railways: In India the railways were not built to have any development on the economical side and were actually built to carry cotton since cotton on dirt makes it dirty to use.The first railway in India was 21 miles long from Bombay(Mumbai) to Thane(Maharashtra). The idea of connecting a railway from Bombay to Thane first occurred to George Clark, The Chief Engineer of the Bombay Government. This thought occurred to him in 1843. Railroads were also used to trade quicker and more efficiently. But doing so resulted in economic conditions and pollution in the country. A person named Lord Dalhousie was the one who came to India to serve as the Governor General of the country and was also the one who convinced the government to build railways in 1848. After 5 years, The first railroad was introduced
